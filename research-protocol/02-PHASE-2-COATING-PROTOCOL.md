# PHASE 2: COATING DEVELOPMENT & CHARACTERIZATION PROTOCOL
## Detailed Experimental Design

**Phase Duration**: 2 months  
**Start**: Month 4 (after Phase 1 completion)  
**Key Deliverable**: Propolis-coated collagen membranes with validated physical/chemical properties

---

## PHASE 2 OVERVIEW

Phase 2 transforms the standardized propolis extract (from Phase 1) into a functional coating on collagen membranes. This phase includes coating protocol development, comprehensive characterization, and release kinetics validation to ensure the coated membrane is suitable for in vitro and in vivo studies.

### Phase 2 Objectives
1. Develop optimal propolis-coating protocol on collagen membranes
2. Comprehensively characterize coating physical and chemical properties
3. Validate mechanical integrity preservation
4. Establish propolis release kinetics and degradation profile
5. Produce validated coated membrane samples for Phase 3

## 2. COATING FORMULATION DESIGN

### 2.1 Coating System Components

**Three-Component Coating System:**

| Component | Function | Specification |
|-----------|----------|---|
| **Propolis Extract** | Bioactive agent | From Phase 1 (standardized, TPC >150 mg GAE/g) |
| **Chitosan** | Carrier/binding matrix | 1% w/v in 1% acetic acid |
| **Stabilizer** | Coating uniformity | Glycerol 1% w/v |

### 2.2 Coating Solution Preparation

**Formulation (Optimized from Literature):**

**Chitosan Base Solution:**
1. Dissolve 1g chitosan powder (low molecular weight, 50-190 kDa, >75% deacetylation) in 100 mL of 1% acetic acid solution
2. Stir gently overnight at room temperature until completely dissolved
3. Solution pH should be 3.5-4.5 (measure with pH meter)
4. Filter through 0.45 μm syringe filter to remove insoluble matter
5. Store at 4°C (stable for 1 week)

**Propolis-Chitosan Coating Solution:**
1. Prepare fresh propolis extract solution: 2% w/v in 70% EtOH (e.g., 20 mg extract in 1 mL solvent)
2. Prepare test formulations with varying propolis concentrations:
   - **Test 1**: 0.5% propolis (5 μL of 2% solution per 1 mL chitosan)
   - **Test 2**: 1.0% propolis (10 μL of 2% solution per 1 mL chitosan)
   - **Test 3**: 1.5% propolis (15 μL of 2% solution per 1 mL chitosan)
   - **Test 4**: 2.0% propolis (20 μL of 2% solution per 1 mL chitosan)
   - **Test 5**: 2.5% propolis (25 μL of 2% solution per 1 mL chitosan)

3. Add 10 μL glycerol per 1 mL coating solution (stabilizer)
4. Mix gently by inversion (avoid bubbles)
5. Allow to equilibrate 30 minutes at room temperature before use
6. Use same day (preparation freshness critical)

**Rationale:**
- **Chitosan**: Cationic polymer with inherent antimicrobial activity; enhances propolis adhesion
- **1% w/v concentration**: Optimal viscosity for even coating application
- **Propolis range (0.5-2.5%)**: Literature-established range; allow optimization
- **Glycerol stabilizer**: Prevents solution phase separation

## 3. COATING METHOD DEVELOPMENT: LAYER-BY-LAYER (LbL) APPROACH

### 3.1 Method Selection Rationale

**Selected Method: Layer-by-Layer (LbL) Electrostatic Assembly**

**Advantages:**
- Precise thickness control (1-5 μm achievable)
- Uniform coating coverage
- Reproducible layer properties
- Minimal membrane damage
- Literature precedent in propolis-collagen studies

**Alternative Method (Backup): Dip-Coating**
- Simpler, faster, no equipment required
- Less precise control but acceptable for initial trials
- Use if LbL unavailable

### 3.2 LbL Coating Procedure

**Equipment:**
- Glass vials or wells (tissue culture plates)
- Tweezers (non-metallic, e.g., plastic or silicone-coated)
- pH meter and buffer solutions
- Distilled water or 0.15 M NaCl wash solution
- Centrifuge (low-speed, optional for drying)

**Coating Protocol (n=15 samples per propolis concentration tested):**

**Step 1: Prepare substrate layers**

- **Layer 1 (Polycation base)**: Poly(allylamine hydrochloride) (PAH)
  - Prepare 0.5 mg/mL PAH in 0.5 M NaCl, pH 7.5
  - Immerse collagen membrane 5 minutes
  - Rinse 2× in water (2 min each)
  - Dry gently with tissue paper
  
- **Layer 2 (Polyanion base)**: Poly(sodium 4-styrenesulfonate) (PSS)
  - Prepare 0.5 mg/mL PSS in 0.5 M NaCl, pH 7.5
  - Immerse 5 minutes
  - Rinse 2× in water (2 min each)
  - Dry gently

**Step 2: Apply propolis-chitosan coating (repeat 3-5 times for multi-layer)**

- **Chitosan layer (polycation)**:
  - Immerse membrane in propolis-chitosan solution 5 minutes
  - Rinse 2× in water (2 min each)
  - Rinse 1× in 0.15 M NaCl
  - Dry gently with tissue

- **PSS layer (polyanion)**:
  - Immerse in PSS solution 5 minutes
  - Rinse 2× in water (2 min each)
  - Rinse 1× in 0.15 M NaCl
  - Dry gently

**Step 3: Final rinse and drying**

- Immerse coated membrane in ultrapure water for 1 minute
- Air dry at room temperature in dark cabinet (2 hours)
- Store at 4°C until testing

**Coating Layer Configuration:**
```
Base: (PAH/PSS)₂
Propolis-active layers: (Chitosan/PSS)₃
Total structure: PAH/PSS/Chitosan-Propolis₁/PSS/Chitosan-Propolis₂/PSS/Chitosan-Propolis₃/PSS
Estimated thickness: 2-5 μm (with 3 propolis-chitosan layers)
```

### 3.3 Dip-Coating Alternative Protocol (if LbL unavailable)

**Simpler but Less Precise:**

1. Hydrate collagen membrane in PBS for 5 minutes
2. Immerse in propolis-chitosan solution for 5 minutes (gentle stirring)
3. Remove and hold vertically to drain excess
4. Air dry completely (2-3 hours at room temperature)
5. Repeat steps 2-4 for 2-3 additional layers (optional, for thicker coating)
6. Final air dry overnight
7. Store at 4°C

**Dip-Coating Result**: Typically 1-3 μm single layer

## 4. COATING OPTIMIZATION: SCREENING PHASE

### 4.1 Experimental Design

**Objective**: Determine optimal propolis concentration for coating

**Test Design:**
- **Variable**: Propolis concentration (0.5%, 1.0%, 1.5%, 2.0%, 2.5%)
- **Replicates**: n=15 samples per concentration (5 for mechanical, 5 for SEM, 5 for FTIR)
- **Duration**: Weeks 1-2 of Phase 2

**Testing Matrix:**

| Sample Group | Propolis Conc. | Coating Method | Tests | n |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P-Coat-0.5 | 0.5% | LbL | Mech, SEM, FTIR, Contact angle | 5 each |
| P-Coat-1.0 | 1.0% | LbL | Mech, SEM, FTIR, Contact angle | 5 each |
| P-Coat-1.5 | 1.5% | LbL | Mech, SEM, FTIR, Contact angle | 5 each |
| P-Coat-2.0 | 2.0% | LbL | Mech, SEM, FTIR, Contact angle | 5 each |
| P-Coat-2.5 | 2.5% | LbL | Mech, SEM, FTIR, Contact angle | 5 each |
| Control (Uncoated) | None | None | Mech, SEM, FTIR, Contact angle | 5 |

### 4.2 Selection Criteria for Optimal Concentration

**Pass criteria for each concentration:**
- ✓ Coating uniformity observed via SEM
- ✓ ≥80% tensile strength retention vs. uncoated
- ✓ FTIR confirms coating deposition (new peaks for polyphenols)
- ✓ Release kinetics show sustained release (not burst >50% in 24h)

**Optimal concentration selected based on:**
1. Best balance of retained mechanical strength + propolis loading
2. Uniform coating morphology (no delamination)
3. Sustained release profile (preferred over burst release)
4. Literature precedent (typically 1.0-1.5% optimal)

## 5. COMPREHENSIVE COATING CHARACTERIZATION

All characterization performed on optimal concentration coating (determined from Section 4.2).

### 5.1 Microscopy & Morphology

#### 5.1.1 Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM)

**Objective**: Visualize coating surface and structure

**Sample Preparation:**
- Mount coated membrane on SEM stubs with conductive adhesive
- Sputter coat with 5 nm gold/palladium
- Store in desiccator until imaging

**Imaging Parameters:**
- Acceleration voltage: 10-15 kV
- Magnification: 1000×, 5000×, 10000× (show progressive detail)
- Resolution: Aim for <50 nm to visualize coating features
- Image locations: 5 images per sample (center, 4 corners)

**Analysis:**
- **Surface morphology**: Smooth (good adhesion) vs. rough (possible delamination)
- **Coating uniformity**: Presence of cracks, holes, or bare membrane spots
- **Particle distribution**: Propolis distribution evenness
- **Coating thickness estimation**: Profile view from sample edge
- **Pore preservation**: Maintain membrane porosity vs. complete coverage

**Expected SEM Results:**
- Smooth, continuous coating without cracks (optimal range)
- Underlying collagen fiber structure visible at 5000×
- Coating thickness: 1-5 μm layer (appears as defined boundary)

#### 5.1.2 Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM, optional)

**Objective**: Ultrastructural visualization of coating layers

**Sample Preparation:**
- Ultra-thin sections (50-100 nm) prepared via microtomy
- Mount on copper grids with carbon film
- Stain with uranyl acetate (optional)

**Imaging:**
- Accelerating voltage: 80-100 kV
- Magnification: 10000-50000×
- Identify individual layers in LbL structure

**Expected Results:**
- Clear demarcation between collagen base and coating layers
- Propolis particles visible as electron-dense inclusions

#### 5.1.3 Optical Microscopy - Thickness Measurement

**Objective**: Precise thickness measurement

**Procedure:**
- Cross-section of coated membrane (fresh, not SEM-prepared)
- Mount on microscope slide
- Use optical microscope with calibrated eyepiece micrometer
- Measure thickness at 5 locations per sample

**Measurement:**
- Baseline collagen thickness: ~75 μm (nominal)
- Coated membrane thickness: ~78-80 μm (for 1-5 μm coating)
- Coating thickness = (coated - uncoated) thickness
- Expected: 1-5 μm coating thickness

---

### 5.2 Spectroscopic Characterization

#### 5.2.1 Fourier-Transform Infrared (FTIR) Spectroscopy

**Objective**: Confirm coating deposition and identify functional groups

**Sample Preparation:**
- Use Attenuated Total Reflectance (ATR) FTIR mode (no special sample prep needed)
- Place coated membrane on ATR crystal
- Measure uncoated collagen as reference

**Measurement Parameters:**
- Wavenumber range: 4000-400 cm⁻¹
- Resolution: 4 cm⁻¹
- Scans: 32 (average)
- Spectral collection: 10 locations per sample

**Expected Spectral Peaks & Assignment:**

| Wavenumber (cm⁻¹) | Assignment | Material |
|---|---|---|
| **1630-1650** | Amide I (C=O stretch) | Collagen (all samples) |
| **1535-1550** | Amide II (N-H bend) | Collagen (all samples) |
| **1200-1300** | Amide III | Collagen (all samples) |
| **1600-1680** | Aromatic C=C stretch | **Propolis (coated only)** |
| **3000-3500** | O-H stretch | Propolis + Collagen |
| **1000-1300** | C-O stretch | Polyphenols (propolis) |

**Quality Indicators:**
- ✓ **Coated samples**: Show NEW peaks in 1600-1680 cm⁻¹ region (aromatic compounds)
- ✓ **Uncoated controls**: Show ONLY amide peaks (no propolis signature)
- ✓ **Coating confirmation**: Distinct spectral difference between coated/uncoated

**Analysis Method:**
- Subtract uncoated spectrum from coated spectrum
- Difference spectrum should show propolis-specific peaks
- Relative peak intensity indicates coating thickness

#### 5.2.2 UV-Vis Spectroscopy (Optional)

**Objective**: Confirm propolis absorption characteristics in coating

**Procedure:**
- Prepare 1 mg/mL coated membrane extract in 70% EtOH (sonication 30 min)
- Measure absorbance 250-400 nm
- Compare to uncoated control and free propolis extract

**Expected Results:**
- Uncoated collagen: Minimal absorption <0.2 AU
- Free propolis: Strong absorption 270-280 nm (phenolic compounds)
- Coated membrane: Intermediate absorption (confirms propolis incorporation)

#### 5.2.3 X-Ray Diffraction (XRD, optional)

**Objective**: Assess crystallinity changes from coating

**Measurement:**
- Powdered collagen membrane (coated and uncoated)
- 2θ range: 10-60°
- Expected peak for collagen Type I: ~20° (broad peak, amorphous)

**Expected Results:**
- Both coated and uncoated: Similar diffraction patterns (no major crystallinity change)
- Coating: Non-crystalline (propolis phenolics typically amorphous)

---

### 5.3 Thermal Analysis

#### 5.3.1 Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC)

**Objective**: Assess thermal stability and thermal properties

**Sample Preparation:**
- Dry coated and uncoated membranes completely (vacuum drying, 24h)
- Weigh ~10 mg into aluminum DSC pan
- Hermetically seal

**Measurement Parameters:**
- Temperature range: 25-300°C
- Heating rate: 10°C/min
- Atmosphere: Nitrogen (40 mL/min)
- Reference: Empty sealed aluminum pan

**Expected Results:**
- **Uncoated collagen**: 
  - Dehydration endotherm ~80-100°C (water loss)
  - Denaturation exotherm ~220-280°C (collagen breakdown)
  
- **Coated membrane**:
  - Similar thermal profile (coating doesn't significantly alter)
  - Slightly higher onset temperature (coating may delay degradation)

**Interpretation:**
- ✓ No significant shift in thermal profile = good coating biocompatibility
- ⚠ Major shift = potential coating-collagen interaction issue

#### 5.3.2 Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA)

**Objective**: Quantify coating mass and thermal degradation

**Sample Preparation:**
- Same as DSC (dried samples, ~10 mg)
- Use platinum or ceramic TGA pan

**Measurement Parameters:**
- Temperature range: 25-500°C
- Heating rate: 10°C/min
- Atmosphere: Nitrogen

**Expected Results & Analysis:**

| Temperature Range | Event | Mass Loss |
|---|---|---|
| **25-100°C** | Water desorption | 5-10% |
| **100-200°C** | Residual solvent removal | 0-5% |
| **200-350°C** | Propolis decomposition (coated only) | **2-8%** (coating mass) |
| **350-500°C** | Collagen decomposition | 20-40% |
| **>500°C** | Ash residue | 2-5% |

**Coating Mass Estimation:**
- TGA mass loss 200-350°C region: Propolis-specific
- Uncoated control: Minimal loss in this region
- **Coating mass % = (Coated mass loss - Uncoated mass loss) / total mass × 100%**
- Expected coating mass: 2-8% w/w

### 5.4 Mechanical Properties Assessment

#### 5.4.1 Tensile Strength Testing

**Objective**: Confirm coating doesn't significantly degrade membrane mechanical properties

**Equipment:**
- Universal mechanical tester (Instron or equivalent)
- Load cell: 100 N capacity
- Environmental chamber: Room temperature (23°C) or 37°C

**Sample Preparation:**
- Cut coated membranes into 10 mm × 50 mm tensile specimens (n=5 per group)
- Hydrate in PBS for 5 minutes before testing
- Mount in grips with 10 mm gauge length
- Measure thickness at 3 locations with micrometer

**Testing Parameters:**
- Temperature: 23°C (room) or 37°C (body temperature, preferred)
- Humidity: 50-65% RH
- Strain rate: 5 mm/min (standard for soft tissues)
- Data acquisition: Force and displacement recorded continuously

**Measurements Extracted:**
- **Tensile Strength (σ)**: Maximum force / cross-sectional area (MPa)
- **Elongation at Break (ε)**: Deformation at rupture (%)
- **Young's Modulus (E)**: Stiffness = stress / strain (MPa)
- **Energy to Break**: Area under stress-strain curve (J/m³)

**Expected Results (Coated vs. Uncoated):**

| Property | Uncoated | Coated (Optimal) | Pass Criterion |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tensile Strength** | 5-15 MPa | 4-13 MPa | ≥80% of control |
| **Elongation at Break** | 50-150% | 45-140% | ≥80% of control |
| **Young's Modulus** | 10-30 MPa | 10-30 MPa | ±20% of control |

**Pass Criterion**: ✓ Coated membrane retains ≥80% tensile strength vs. uncoated

#### 5.4.2 Tear Resistance

**Objective**: Assess coating impact on membrane tear properties

**Procedure (Using Tensile Tester):**
- Cut samples with notch (Trouser Tear Test configuration)
- Measure tear force and elongation at tear propagation
- Expected: Coating should not significantly reduce tear resistance

### 5.5 Hydrophilicity & Surface Properties

#### 5.5.1 Contact Angle Measurement

**Objective**: Assess coating impact on membrane wettability

**Procedure:**
- Sessile drop method using contact angle goniometer
- Place 10 μL ultrapure water drop on horizontal surface
- Measure contact angle at 1, 5, and 10 seconds
- Perform at 5 locations per sample (n=5 samples)

**Expected Results:**

| Coating | Contact Angle (°) | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| **Uncoated collagen** | 45-65° | Hydrophilic (native collagen) |
| **Coated membrane** | 35-55° | More hydrophilic (coating enhances water uptake) |

**Interpretation:**
- ✓ Decreased contact angle = improved hydrophilicity
- Beneficial for cell interactions and aqueous propolis release

#### 5.5.2 Water Uptake / Swelling

**Objective**: Quantify coating effect on water absorption

**Procedure:**
1. Dry coated membrane completely (vacuum 24h)
2. Record initial dry weight (m_dry) to ±0.001 mg
3. Immerse in ultrapure water or PBS at 37°C
4. Remove at time points: 5 min, 30 min, 1h, 4h, 24h
5. Blot excess water with tissue (standard time, 5 sec)
6. Record wet weight (m_wet)
7. Calculate water uptake % = [(m_wet - m_dry) / m_dry] × 100%

**Expected Results:**
- Uncoated collagen: 200-300% water uptake (highly hydrophilic)
- Coated membrane: 150-250% water uptake (coating slightly reduces, but still good)
- **Acceptable range**: ≥100% water uptake for osteoblast culture

## 6. PROPOLIS RELEASE KINETICS & DEGRADATION STUDIES

### 6.1 Release Profile Determination

**Objective**: Characterize propolis release from coated membrane into aqueous medium

**Duration**: 4 weeks (28 days) of continuous monitoring

#### 6.1.1 Experimental Setup

**Test Conditions:**
- **Medium**: Phosphate-Buffered Saline (PBS), pH 7.4, physiological osmolarity
- **Temperature**: 37°C (body temperature)
- **Volume**: 10 mL PBS per sample
- **Container**: Sterile 15 mL polypropylene tubes
- **Sample size**: n=3 replicates per timepoint

**Experimental Design:**

| Timepoint | 1h | 4h | 8h | 24h | 3d | 7d | 14d | 21d | 28d |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sample collection** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ |
| **Fresh medium addition** | - | - | - | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ |

**Protocol:**
1. Prepare coated membrane samples (10 mm × 10 mm, n=3 per group)
2. Place in labeled sterile tubes with 10 mL PBS (37°C)
3. At each timepoint:
   - Remove 1 mL aliquot, store at -20°C until analysis
   - Add 1 mL fresh PBS to maintain volume
   - Incubate at 37°C with gentle stirring

#### 6.1.2 Propolis Quantification via HPLC

**Objective**: Quantify total propolis phenolics released at each timepoint

**Analysis Method:**
- Use HPLC system from Phase 1 QC protocol
- Analyze release medium at each timepoint
- Quantify total phenolic equivalents (TPC) released
- Alternative: Quantify specific marker compounds (caffeic acid, etc.)

**Calculation of Cumulative Release:**
- **Cumulative release (%) = [Total phenolics at timepoint / Total phenolics in coating] × 100%**
- Example:
  - Initial propolis in coating: 50 mg TPC equivalents
  - At 24h: 15 mg TPC found in release medium
  - Release at 24h = (15/50) × 100% = 30%

#### 6.1.3 Data Presentation & Analysis

**Release Profile Graph:**
- X-axis: Time (hours, then days)
- Y-axis: Cumulative propolis release (%)
- Plot with error bars (mean ± SD, n=3)
- Expected pattern: 

```
Cumulative Release %
100%  │                              ●
      │                            ●
 80%  │                          ●
      │                        ●
 60%  │                      ●
      │                    ●
 40%  │                  ●
      │                ●
 20%  │              ●
      │            ●
  0%  └─────────────────────────────→ Time (days)
      0    1    3    7    14   21   28
```

**Release Profile Classification:**

| Profile Type | Description | Initial 24h Release | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Burst Release** | High initial release | >40% | Complete by day 7 |
| **Sustained Release** | Gradual linear release | 20-40% | Over 4 weeks |
| **Biphasic** | Initial burst + sustained | 25-35% (24h) | 60-80% by day 28 |

**Expected Result**: Biphasic or sustained release (preferred over burst) — propolis released over 2-4 weeks

### 6.2 Degradation Study

**Objective**: Assess coating and membrane degradation over time

**Duration**: 8-12 weeks

#### 6.2.1 Mass Loss Assessment

**Procedure:**
1. Prepare coated membrane samples (10 mm × 10 mm, triplicate)
2. Record initial dry mass (m₀) after vacuum drying 24h
3. Incubate in PBS, 37°C
4. At timepoints (1, 2, 4, 8, 12 weeks):
   - Remove samples
   - Rinse in ultrapure water
   - Dry to constant weight (vacuum oven, 24h)
   - Record mass (mₜ)
   - Return to fresh PBS

**Calculation:**
- **Mass loss (%) = [(m₀ - mₜ) / m₀] × 100%**

**Expected Results:**

| Week | Uncoated Collagen | Coated Membrane |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | 100% | 100% |
| **2** | 95% | 97% |
| **4** | 85% | 90% |
| **8** | 50% | 65% |
| **12** | 20% | 35% |

**T½ (Half-life) Calculation:**
- Time at which mass loss = 50%
- Expected: Uncoated ~6-8 weeks; Coated ~8-10 weeks
- **Coating should slightly slow degradation** (beneficial for GBR timeline)

#### 6.2.2 Mechanical Property Degradation

**Procedure:**
- At 0, 2, 4, 8 weeks, test tensile strength of degrading samples
- Follow protocol from Section 5.4.1
- Plot strength vs. time

**Expected Results:**
- Gradual decrease in tensile strength correlating with mass loss
- Coated membrane maintains slightly higher strength longer

#### 6.2.3 SEM Morphology Evolution

**Procedure:**
- Take SEM images of samples at 0, 4, 8, 12 weeks
- Observe surface degradation progression
- Document coating delamination (if any) over time

**Expected Observations:**
- Early timepoints (0-4 weeks): Uniform coating maintained
- Mid timepoints (4-8 weeks): Beginning surface erosion
- Late timepoints (8-12 weeks): Progressive coating breakdown, collagen fibers becoming exposed
